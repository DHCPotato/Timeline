1949

First Society Meeting

Led by Mr. McPerson of the American Humane Association, the meeting addressed key topics, concluding with the selection of "Humane Society of the Pikes Peak Region" as the name and confirming Mr. Wickes as president.

1950

Purchase of the First Shelter Property

Cragmor Road was designated as the official location, originally serving as kennels for the nearby greyhound racetrack. A dedicated group of primarily female volunteers took charge of animal pickups throughout the city using a 1937 Ford. Also, Mr. Abbot was voted in as president by a landslide.

1950

Muffin Finds a Home!

Our very first adoption! A sweet poodle mix named Muffin was welcomed into the loving home of Gen. Finley and his wife—marking the beginning of many happy tails to come.

1951

610 Abbot Ln. Became Home

The contract for the new shelter building was finalized, with construction scheduled to begin on December 17th. Plans for seeding the farmland were also confirmed. The property, spanning 8 acres, was purchased for $11,000, bringing the total cost, including all expenses, to $25,096.84.

1954

Paws on the Air!

A TV show was started on KRDO as a public service, featuring adoptable animals from HSPPR. This partnership has continued well beyond the 1950s!

1955

Paws Off the Produce!

The City of Colorado Springs asked HSPPR to begin enforcing leash laws. This law was initially put into effect during WWII, as dogs were digging up the public’s home gardens that became popular due to rationing.

1956

Hammer, Nails, and Happy Tails

Construction kicked off on HSPPR’s very first veterinary clinic, along with a much-needed kennel expansion—making room for more wagging tails and helping paws! This was a big step forward in providing better care for the animals who needed it most.

1958

Tag You're It!

The City of Colorado Springs gave HSPPR the green light to keep the revenue from dog licensing—a pawsitively helpful decision that supported our mission and helped more furry friends get the care, shelter, and love they deserved.

1962

Pawperty Upgrade Complete!

Another exciting expansion of the shelter began—and was successfully completed! With new office space, added kennels, outdoor dog runs, small animal areas, and fresh concrete replacing dirt roads, HSPPR continued to grow its ability to care for the community’s furry friends in need.

1966

Rabies Panic Hits Fort Carson

Following a suspected rabies outbreak, the shelter helped calm fears, quarantine pets, and publish a study that shaped future policies around outbreaks and pet safety.

1967

Seabolt becomes Executive

The contract for the new shelter building was finalized, with construction scheduled to begin on December 17th. Plans for seeding the farmland were also confirmed. The property, spanning 8 acres, was purchased for $11,000, bringing the total cost, including all expenses, to $25,096.84.

1972

From Leashes to Legacy

Martin Luther 'Skip' Seabolt, hired in 1953, was vital to HSPPR’s growth, managing shelter operations 24/7 and living on-site with his family. He served 20 years.

1973

Here Comes the Pet-Mobile!

Phil Arkow became the education and publicity manager. He did news media, school lectures, tours, and visited nursing homes. He became a significant figure for HSPPR in the years to come. He also started a pet therapy program that brought animals to visit people with disabilities in a van, called the “Pet-Mobile.

1976

Ron Hunter becomes Executive

The contract for the new shelter building was finalized, with construction scheduled to begin on December 17th. Plans for seeding the farmland were also confirmed. The property, spanning 8 acres, was purchased for $11,000, bringing the total cost, including all expenses, to $25,096.84.

1976

Snips and Stamps

HSPPR is recognized as having one of the top spay/neuter programs in the US. We also made a TV commercial with USPS relating to the problem of dog bites and mailmen.

1982

A Bow for Mr. Abbot

Mr. Abbot Stepped down from the board and received the title of “President Emeritus” and was voted honorary member of the board of directors.

1983

Fur-Ever Rides

Phil Long Ford partnered with us to feature several adorable shelter animals in their advertisements—and generously covered the cost of their adoptions, helping them find loving homes

1989

5,000 Tails Told

HSPPR proudly reached a major milestone this year—5,000 animals adopted into loving homes! This incredible achievement reflects the dedication of our staff, volunteers, and community in giving thousands of pets a second chance.

1993

Dr.Wesley Metzler becomes Executive

1998

Fur Ball Beginnings

The very first Fur Ball event—originally called the 'Furrr Ball Gala'—was a huge success, raising an impressive $25,000 to support the animals and programs at HSPPR. This elegant evening marked the beginning of what would become one of our most cherished and impactful annual fundraisers.

1999

Digging Into the Future

In April, we officially broke ground on our brand-new shelter—a huge step forward in our mission to care for more animals in need. This exciting milestone marks the beginning of a brighter, more spacious future for pets and the people who love them

2000

Paws Are In the House!

We officially opened the doors to our brand-new shelter—a warm, welcoming space designed to provide even better care for animals in need. This exciting milestone means more wagging tails, happy purrs, and second chances for pets in our community.

2002

HSPPR Heads South

HSPPR proudly expanded its reach by opening a new location in Pueblo, bringing our mission of compassion, care, and second chances to even more pets and people in Pueblo.

2006

Top Dog Status!

HSPPR was honored with the distinction of being the highest-rated organization ever reviewed by the National Animal Control Association (NACA)—a proud moment that reflects our commitment to excellence in animal care, public safety, and community service.

2008

Tails, Traps & Triumph

HSPPR launched its very own Trap-Neuter-Return (TNR) program to help manage and care for community cats. This humane initiative focuses on reducing overpopulation while giving outdoor cats a healthier, safer life in the neighborhoods they call home.

2009

Pawtoberfest: Ale & Tails

Once a K9-5K and festival, Pawtoberfest has grown into one of Colorado Springs’ most beloved dog-friendly beer tasting events. With new locations over the years and thousands of attendees, it blends brews, community, and compassion—all while raising funds for animals in need. Its popularity even earned a spot on Animal Planet’s list of the Top 10 Dog Festivals nationwide.

2012

Snips and Rescues, HSPPR Style

HSPPR spayed and neutered over 10,000 animals in one year. Over 1,200 animals were sheltered during the Black Forest Fire. ALE performed search and rescue, saving 680 animals from the evacuation zone.

2014

Hearts at Work

HSPPR has officially surpassed an incredible milestone by reaching a total of 100,000 volunteer hours contributed by dedicated individuals who have generously given their time to support the organization’s mission.

2018

Lights, Camera, Action!

In 2018, Rocky Mountain Animal Rescue on Nat Geo WILD spotlighted HSPPR’s ALE team and vet clinic. Though it only ran one season, fans of the show still visit today—moved by the mission they saw on screen.

2022

Bigger Hearts, Bigger Clinic

The Colorado Springs veterinary clinic has expanded its facilities to better serve the community, and a brand-new foster space has been added to provide a warm, welcoming environment for animals awaiting their forever homes.

2023

Strengthening the Front Lines

Thanks to generous community donations, HSPPR completed an $850,000 remodel of the Animal Law Enforcement headquarters. The upgrades added secure offices, storage, and dispatch space—ensuring officers can better protect animals and people across 5,000 square miles.

Barking Up Memory Lane
